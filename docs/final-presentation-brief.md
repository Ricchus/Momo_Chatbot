# Final Presentation Brief

This document turns the current project evidence into a presentation-ready narrative. It separates confirmed facts from open gaps so the final deck can stay accurate and defensible.

## 1. Project Arc

### Midpoint checkpoint

- A midpoint-check presentation was created as the project checkpoint artifact.
- This marks the transition from early framing into concept generation and prototype development.

### Concept generation after midpoint

- 30 concepts were generated based on the design requirements.
- Gemini was used to produce concept sketches for these ideas.
- Output artifacts: concept set, sketch images.

### Concept evaluation and narrowing

- Three rounds of peer voting/scoring were conducted with HCI and Industrial Design peers.
- Feasibility screening was also applied during the evaluation process.
- Results were summarized in an Excel file covering all three rounds.
- The process shortlisted Concept `#2`, Concept `#8`, and Concept `#19`.

### Final concept selection

- Concept `#8` was selected from the shortlist.
- The shortlist itself came from peer voting plus feasibility filtering.
- The final choice of `#8` was based on personal preference after the shortlist stage.

### UX articulation for Concept #8

- A user journey comparison was produced to contrast the avatar chatbot with a traditional chatbot UI.
- Output artifact: screenshot comparing the two user journeys.

### Prototype communication

- A demo video was produced to show the prototype in action.
- Output artifact: prototype demo video.

### Avatar identity selection

- Multiple avatar options were designed and shown in an "avatar pick" questionnaire.
- HCI classmates participated in the selection process.
- The final avatar identity chosen was a bird.

### Avatar behavior design

- The avatar was implemented as GIF-based animation states.
- GIF switching logic was designed so the avatar changes action and expression during conversation.
- Output artifacts: GIF asset set plus switching logic.

### Prototype feedback and iteration

- A prototype walkthrough was recorded.
- A questionnaire was used to collect feedback from HCI and Industrial Design peers.
- Five comments were collected and used to guide iteration.
- Output artifacts: walkthrough recording, questionnaire, five comments, iterated prototype.

### Evaluation study design

- The study was designed to evaluate whether the avatar chatbot reduces workplace stress more effectively than a traditional UI.
- Each participant completes two rounds of the same simulated office workflow:
  - one with ChatGPT / traditional chatbot UI
  - one with the Avatar Assistant
- The order of the two tools is counterbalanced.

### IRB-facing procedure refinement

- The procedure supports two participation modalities:
  - in person at a Georgia Tech workstation
  - remote through Prolific
- Both modalities use the same web-based experimental program.
- Participants are reminded to use fictional materials only and not enter real personal or sensitive information.
- Participants are also told not to use personal accounts such as their own email or calendar.

### Study stimuli production

- Fictional office-workflow materials were created, including emails and tasks.
- The business background was revised to be less tech-heavy so non-tech participants could understand it more easily.
- A final background option was selected.
- Materials were translated into English and exported to Word.

## 2. Core Design Requirements Confirmed So Far
